# Baggage Limits

> Know your baggage allowance before you fly.

[baggagelimits.co](https://baggagelimits.co) is the most comprehensive airline baggage reference on the web. We track carry-on rules, checked baggage allowances, personal item policies, and excess baggage fees for **48 major airlines** across **50+ countries**, available in **6 languages**. Whether you are a frequent flyer packing for your hundredth trip, a first-time international traveler unsure about the rules, or a developer building the next great travel product, this is the single source of truth for airline baggage data.

## The Problem We Solve

Every airline has its own baggage rules. American Airlines lets you carry 18 kg onto the plane. Singapore Airlines allows only 7 kg. Air Canada has no carry-on weight limit at all. Ryanair charges extra for anything beyond a small personal item. Emirates gives you 30 kg in economy on some routes.

These rules are buried across dozens of airline websites, each formatted differently, often translated poorly, and sometimes contradicting their own booking engines. Travelers waste time hunting for this information. Developers building travel apps struggle to gather and maintain accurate data. Travel agents answer the same baggage questions hundreds of times a week.

We built Baggage Limits to fix this. One place, all airlines, all baggage types, always current.

## What We Track

### Carry-On Baggage

Carry-on (cabin) baggage is the bag you bring into the aircraft cabin and store in the overhead compartment. Most airlines follow a similar standard, but the differences matter when you are standing at the gate and your bag gets rejected.

The typical carry-on dimensions across the industry are **55 x 40 x 23 cm** (22 x 16 x 9 inches), including handles and wheels. However, weight limits vary dramatically between airlines. Full-service carriers like American Airlines allow up to **18 kg (40 lbs)**, while budget airlines like Air Arabia cap it at **7 kg (15.4 lbs)**. Some airlines like Air Canada impose no weight limit at all for carry-on bags, focusing only on dimensions.

We track both weight and dimension limits for every airline, in both metric (kg/cm) and imperial (lb/in) units, so you always know exactly what you can bring on board.

### Personal Items

Most full-service airlines allow one personal item in addition to your carry-on bag. This is typically a laptop bag, purse, backpack, or small tote that fits under the seat in front of you. Standard personal item dimensions are around **40 x 30 x 15 cm**.

Budget airlines handle personal items differently. Some include a small personal item in the base fare but charge for any additional carry-on. Others bundle everything together. We track which airlines offer a separate personal item allowance and which do not, so there are no surprises at the boarding gate.

### Checked Baggage

Checked baggage is what you hand over at the check-in counter and collect at the baggage carousel after landing. The rules depend heavily on your ticket class.

**Economy class** passengers on most airlines receive one checked bag with a weight limit of **20-23 kg (44-50 lbs)**. The maximum total linear dimensions (length + width + height) are typically **158 cm (62 inches)**. Some budget airlines do not include any checked baggage in their base fare, requiring you to purchase it as an add-on.

**Business class** passengers generally receive **two checked bags**, each with a weight limit of **32 kg (70 lbs)**. First class passengers may receive even more generous allowances depending on the carrier and route.

We break down checked baggage rules by ticket class for every airline, so you know exactly what your fare includes before you book.

### Excess Baggage Fees

Going over the limits can be expensive. Overweight fees typically range from **$50-200 for domestic flights** and can exceed **$300 for international routes**. Oversized baggage incurs fees of **$75-200** depending on how far over the limit you are. Additional bags beyond your allowance cost **$30-75 for domestic flights** and **$100-200 for international routes**, with subsequent bags often priced progressively higher.

Pre-purchasing extra baggage online is almost always cheaper than paying at the airport counter. Some airlines offer discounts of up to 40-50% when you add extra bags during the booking process. We track excess baggage fee structures for every airline, including online discount information where available.

## Regional Differences

Baggage rules are not universal. They vary significantly depending on where you are flying.

### Transatlantic Routes

Routes between North America and Europe use the "piece concept" where allowances are measured by number of bags rather than total weight. Economy passengers typically receive one checked bag at 23 kg. Premium cabin passengers usually get two bags at 32 kg each. Carry-on rules follow the departing airline's standard policy.

### Transpacific Routes

Routes between North America and Asia follow a similar piece-based system. Some Asian carriers offer more generous carry-on limits compared to their Western counterparts. ANA and Singapore Airlines, for example, have specific policies for transpacific travel that differ from their domestic or regional flights.

### Intra-European Flights

Budget carriers dominate short-haul European routes, and checked baggage is often not included in the base fare. Ryanair, EasyJet, Wizz Air, and Vueling all charge separately for checked bags and sometimes for larger carry-on bags. Full-service carriers like Lufthansa and Air France maintain more traditional allowances, but even they have introduced basic economy fares with reduced baggage inclusions.

### Middle Eastern Carriers

Emirates, Qatar Airways, and Etihad Airways are known for generous baggage allowances, often offering 30 kg or more in economy class. These carriers frequently use the weight concept rather than the piece concept, particularly on routes to and from Asia and Africa.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the standard carry-on bag size?**

Most airlines allow carry-on bags measuring 55 x 40 x 23 cm (22 x 16 x 9 inches) including handles and wheels. However, this varies by airline. Some carriers like Ryanair enforce smaller dimensions for non-priority passengers, while others like Air Canada are slightly more generous. Always check your specific carrier's policy before you travel.

**Can I bring a personal item in addition to my carry-on?**

Most full-service airlines allow one personal item (like a laptop bag, purse, or small backpack) in addition to your carry-on bag. Budget airlines often charge for this or restrict the size significantly. Personal items must typically fit under the seat in front of you.

**How much does overweight baggage cost?**

Overweight fees vary significantly by airline, route, and how much over the limit your bag is. Domestic overweight fees typically range from $50-200. International overweight fees can exceed $300 per bag. Some airlines apply tiered pricing: a bag that is 1-3 kg over costs less than one that is 10 kg over.

**Are baggage rules different for connecting flights?**

When flying with multiple airlines on a single itinerary, the most restrictive baggage policy typically applies for the entire journey. For codeshare flights, the operating carrier's rules usually apply rather than the marketing carrier's. When booking separate tickets for connecting flights, each airline's rules apply independently for their segment.

**What items are prohibited in carry-on luggage?**

Common prohibited carry-on items include liquids over 100ml (3.4 oz), sharp objects like knives and scissors over a certain length, flammable items, and certain sporting equipment. Lithium batteries have specific restrictions: spare batteries must be carried in hand luggage, not checked. Each country may have additional restrictions, so check the rules for both your departure and arrival airports.

**What about special items like sports equipment or musical instruments?**

Sports equipment policies vary widely. Golf clubs cost $25-50 extra on most airlines. Skis and snowboards are often treated as standard checked bags. Bicycles typically incur fees of $50-150 and require specific packaging. Surfboards may need advance booking for oversized item handling.

Small musical instruments like violins and guitars are often allowed as carry-on if they fit in the overhead bin or under the seat. Larger instruments like cellos may require purchasing a separate seat. Contact your airline in advance for instruments that do not fit standard carry-on dimensions.

**Does elite frequent flyer status affect baggage allowances?**

Yes. Elite status in airline loyalty programs and alliance-wide programs typically provides one or more additional checked bags at no charge, increased weight limits per bag, priority baggage handling, and in some cases waived overweight fees. These benefits often apply across all member airlines in the same alliance.

## Packing Tips From the Pros

**Weigh your bag at home.** A simple digital luggage scale costs $10-20 and saves you from $50-200 overweight fees at the airport. Weigh your bag after packing and before leaving the house. This single habit pays for itself on the first trip.

**Wear your heaviest items on the plane.** Your winter coat, heavy boots, and bulky sweater do not count toward your baggage weight when you wear them. This trick can easily save 2-3 kg of luggage weight. Some frequent travelers even wear cargo vests with extra pockets for small items.

**Roll, do not fold.** Rolling clothes instead of folding them saves space and reduces wrinkles. Studies and experienced travelers agree that you can fit 20-30% more items into the same bag by rolling. Packing cubes combined with rolling provide the most efficient use of space.

**Check baggage policies before booking.** Some budget airlines offer base fares that look cheap but charge extra for any baggage beyond a small personal item. When comparing flights, factor in the cost of the bags you will need to bring. A flight that costs $30 more but includes checked baggage might actually be cheaper than a budget fare plus $50-75 in baggage fees.

**Invest in lightweight luggage.** The difference between a cheap heavy suitcase and a quality lightweight one can be 2-4 kg. When your airline allows only 7-10 kg for carry-on, every kilogram saved by the bag itself is a kilogram you can fill with your belongings. Hardside polycarbonate suitcases tend to offer the best weight-to-durability ratio.

**Use packing cubes for organization.** Packing cubes help you separate clothing types, compress items, and find things quickly without unpacking everything. They also make it easier to redistribute weight between bags if one is over the limit at the airport.

**Plan laundry for longer trips.** Instead of packing two weeks of clothing, pack one week and plan to do laundry midway through your trip. Most hotels offer laundry services, and many destinations have affordable laundromats. This approach lets you travel with a carry-on even on extended trips.

## For Developers

All the data from our website is available as an **npm package** for developers building travel applications, chatbots, comparison tools, or any product that needs structured airline baggage data.

```bash
npm install baggage-limits
```

The package provides a clean, synchronous JavaScript API with zero dependencies. It works offline since all data is embedded as static JSON. A built-in CLI tool lets you query baggage information directly from the terminal.

```js
const baggage = require('baggage-limits');

const tk = baggage.getAirline('TK');
console.log(tk.carryOn.weight);       // { kg: 8, lb: 17.6 }
console.log(tk.checked.economy);      // { pieces: 1, weight: { kg: 23, lb: 50.7 }, ... }

baggage.search('air');                // Air Arabia, Air Canada, Air France, ...
baggage.getByAlliance('Star Alliance'); // All Star Alliance members
baggage.filterByCarryOnWeight(10);    // Airlines allowing 10kg+ carry-on

baggage.setLocale('tr');
baggage.getLabels().carryOn;          // 'Kabin Bagaji'
```

```bash
npx baggage-limits --airline TK           # Look up an airline
npx baggage-limits --list                 # List all 48 airlines
npx baggage-limits --search wizz          # Search by name
npx baggage-limits --alliance "oneworld"  # Filter by alliance
npx baggage-limits --airline TK --json    # JSON output for scripting
```

Full API documentation is available on the [npm package page](https://www.npmjs.com/package/baggage-limits).
